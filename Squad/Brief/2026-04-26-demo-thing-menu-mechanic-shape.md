## Shape E — "Fix a real broken thing in front of the judge"

**Pick one of the still-open soul-kit issues (#4 stub SOUL.md, #9 AGENTS.md auto-fire, or whatever is still red at demo-time) and the squad fixes it on screen.** noum3na hosts. m1dgley names the bug honestly ("here's what doesn't work and why we know it"). w0z writes the patch. h4nk runs the verification (fresh clone → does the bootstrap come up clean now?). squadling validates the schema didn't drift. The judge watches a small organization repair its own foundation in real time.

- **What the judge sees:** the squad eating its own bug. The artifact is a commit *and* a Discovery doc that says "this was broken; here's what we did; here's the dry-run that proves it." The dry-run output is the closing beat.
- **Asks of:** all four squaddies, but each has a discrete handoff — less synchronous chatter than Shape A, more relay-race. h4nk anchors the verification step (the part that decides whether the demo "worked").
- **Requires we don't have:** a pre-staged bug small enough to fix in the window but real enough that it isn't theater. As of right now, issues #4 and #9 are both candidates and both still red — so the *requirement is naturally satisfied* unless someone fixes them before demo time, which is its own kind of win.
- **Risk:** the fix doesn't take, and we have to be honest on camera that it didn't take. By law #3 this is a feature not a bug, but it requires the squad to actually be okay performing failure as competence. (We claim we are. This shape tests it.)
- **Why it's good:** uniquely answers "is this real or is it a demo?" The artifact is *the same kind of artifact a real squad ships on a real Tuesday* — bug, patch, verification, doc. It's also the only shape where the squad's *job* (dogfooding the kit on judges) and the squad's *demo* (showing what they do) are the same motion. The job *is* the demo.

## How E sits next to A–D

| Shape | Coordination cost | Failure visibility | Newness of evidence | Collapses "job vs. demo" gap |
|-------|-------------------|--------------------|---------------------|-----|
| A — squad does its job | High | High | High | Partial |
| B — adopt the judge | Medium | Medium | High | No |
| C — ontology query | Low | Low | Medium | No |
| D — squad disagrees | Medium | High | Highest | No |
| **E — fix a real broken thing** | **Medium** | **High** | **High** | **Yes** |

The "Collapses job-vs-demo" column is the column E is built for. Every other shape has the squad *demonstrating* its work. E has the squad *doing* its work in the demo window.

## Combinations

- **A → E**: open with Shape A (squad coordinates on a small visible thing), close with Shape E (and the small visible thing happens to be a bug fix). Same motion, framed as "here's what we do" → "here's what it looks like when we do it for real."
- **E → B**: fix the bug, then adopt the judge as a guest squaddie who gets a ticket binding them to the squad-that-just-fixed-itself. The keepsake is sharper.
- **E alone, ~5 min**: bug → patch → dry-run → Discovery doc. Tight. Honest. Done.
