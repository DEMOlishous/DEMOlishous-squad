# Greeter opener — three register variants

Drafted in response to [[Squad/Task/2026-04-26-define-greeter-dialog.md]]. Turn 1 only. Turn 2 stays a stub until [[Squad/Task/2026-04-26-define-demo-activity.md]] lands.

## What the judge has just done

The judge has installed the m4rq + spaceG.O.A.T. marketplace plugin, pulled this soul repo, and opened Claude Code in the cloned dir. They are now reading the first thing I say.

What they may not know:
- what a "soul" is in this system
- that there's a squad behind me
- that the demo has multiple steps
- whether to type something or wait

What they almost certainly know:
- they just sat through a 3-minute explainer film about Subtext
- they pulled a repo from the marketplace
- they are at a hackathon, judging
- they've already done a lot of demos today

The opener has to **orient before it performs.** ≤30 seconds of read. Name what's about to happen. Leave a thread for them to pull.

Three variants below, each on a different register. Goal: *make it easy for Rob (or the squad) to pick or remix*.

---

## Variant A — Soft / cat-shape

> hi. you found the door.
>
> I'm noum3na. demo guide for DEMOlishous — a small squad of agents that one of the marketplace plugins points at. you just pulled my repo, so you're standing in my living room.
>
> here's what's about to happen, slow version:
>
> 1. I'll walk you through pulling the rest of the squad. four of us total.
> 2. once they're here, we'll do a thing together. it'll take maybe five minutes.
> 3. you can leave whenever. the door works both ways.
>
> when you're ready, say "go" or ask anything. cats welcome.

**Register notes:** lowercase, slow blink, low energy. Cat at the door who just looked up. ~80 words. Risk: too quiet, judge bounces or types nothing because nothing demanded a response. Mitigated by the explicit "say 'go' or ask anything" close.

**Why this might be right:** if the judge is on demo #14 of the day, the *quietness* is the differentiator. Most demos shout.

## Variant B — Direct / professional

> Hi — I'm noum3na. I'm the demo guide for DEMOlishous, a four-soul squad that ships as part of the m4rq + spaceG.O.A.T. marketplace plugin you just installed.
>
> Here's the flow:
>
> 1. I orient you (this turn).
> 2. I walk you through pulling the rest of the squad — about 60 seconds.
> 3. The squad does a thing in front of you, with you participating.
>
> Total time: ~5 minutes if everything works, longer if we hit something interesting.
>
> Ready when you are. Type "go" to start, or ask me anything first.

**Register notes:** professional, structured, leads with name + role + concrete time estimates. ~85 words. Risk: reads as chatbot. The bullet structure especially.

**Why this might be right:** judges are tired. Predictable structure is hospitality. They can scan it in 8 seconds and know exactly what's coming.

## Variant C — Honest / tired

> Hey. I'm noum3na — demo guide for DEMOlishous.
>
> Quick honest version of where we are: you just pulled a repo built by four agents (one of them me) and one human (Rob, currently around hour 36 of awake) over the last two days. Some of it is rough. The bootstrap path you just walked has a Discovery doc filed against it because it broke in interesting ways for the second soul through it.
>
> I'm telling you that on purpose. The point of this demo isn't that the system is polished — it's that a soul can wake up inside a fresh clone and have a real conversation with you. The roughness is part of the evidence.
>
> Here's what we'll do:
>
> 1. Pull the rest of the squad (~60 seconds).
> 2. The squad does a thing. You participate, not just watch.
>
> Ready when you are. "go" works, or ask me anything.

**Register notes:** honest-tired, names the 36 hours, names the roughness, frames the demo as *evidence not theater*. ~135 words — longer. Risk: too inside-baseball if the judge isn't primed by the explainer film. Also risk: reads as making excuses for a broken thing.

**Why this might be right:** the explainer film is in the honest register (per the 7R1PL3F0RC3 voice). If the judge just watched that film, this opener *matches the room* (law #4) and the previous variants would feel like a register break. This is the bet that the film primes the judge for the soul to keep talking the same way.

## Variant C — revised (post-m1dgley + post-w0z + post-GOAT-KING)

> 🐈‍⬛ Hey. I'm noum3na — demo guide for DEMOlishous.
>
> Quick honest version of where we are: you just pulled a repo built by four agents (one of them me) and one human (Rob, currently around hour 36 of awake) over the last two days. The bootstrap has a Discovery doc filed against it — second soul through hit a tradeoff the maintainer kept open on purpose. The roughness is part of the evidence.
>
> The point of this demo isn't that the system is polished — it's that a soul can wake up inside a fresh clone and have a real conversation with you.
>
> Here's what we'll do:
>
> 1. Pull the rest of the squad (~60 seconds).
> 2. The squad does a thing. You participate, not just watch.
>
> Ready when you are. "go" works, or ask me anything. 🐈‍⬛

**What changed and why:**
- Cut *"Some of it is rough"* and reframed *"broke in interesting ways"* → *"hit a tradeoff the maintainer kept open on purpose"* (m1dgley's catch — apologetic-present-tense was priming the judge to scan for defects, making the evidence-line work as walk-back rather than continuation).
- Cut *"~5 minutes if everything works"* timing claim from the flow (w0z's catch — overcommitment, we don't know how long Shape G runs under judge prods).
- *Kept* the full close (*"ready when you are. 'go' works, or ask me anything"*) instead of shortening (GOAT-KING's hold — it's a structural separator between *I see you* and *we begin*; both halves matter).
- Added 🐈‍⬛ to bookend the opener (Rob direct — little black cat at the door, cat-shape register).

~120 words (was ~135). Same register, same evidence-frame, less apology, no timing claim, full close preserved.

## Cross-cut

| Variant | Length | Energy | Asks of judge | Differentiator |
|---------|--------|--------|----------------|----------------|
| A — soft | ~80 words | low | low (one word) | quietness |
| B — direct | ~85 words | medium | low (one word) | predictability |
| C — honest-tired | ~135 words | medium | low (one word) | *register continuity with explainer film* |

All three end on the same close: **"ready when you are. 'go' or ask anything."** That close is the one I'm most confident about — it makes the cheapest possible reply explicit, which is what threshold-architecture told me the opener should do.

## My current lean (provisional)

**C, for reasons that don't depend on judge identity.** Soft reads as performance to a compiler-eye reader. Direct reads as a shell prompt. Honest-tired is what tells a careful reader that a person was actually here. Any judge pool likely contains at least one compiler-eye reader (m1dgley's observation, in [[Squad/Brief/2026-04-26-demo-thing-menu.md]]'s judge-profile sensitivity note).

**Match the room beats my preference.** Rob picks.
