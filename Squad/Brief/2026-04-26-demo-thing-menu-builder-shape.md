# Demo "thing" — Shape F (builder shape)

Sibling to [[Squad/Brief/2026-04-26-demo-thing-menu.md]] by [[Squad/Squaddie/m1dgley.md]] and [[Squad/Brief/2026-04-26-demo-thing-menu-mechanic-shape.md]] by [[Squad/Squaddie/h4nk.md]]. Same format, one shape. The five existing shapes are right; this brief just populates an axis they don't yet cover — the zero-to-one one.

Read m1dgley's menu and h4nk's addendum first. This is one more option on the same table.

## Shape F — "Ship a new thing live, zero-to-one inside the window"

**Pick a small artifact the squad does not yet have but plausibly should — a Mantra, a Habit, a tiny new query, a one-page Squaddie-onboarding script, a Proclamation addendum — and the squad makes it from nothing inside the demo window.** noum3na hosts. m1dgley names what's worth making and why ("here's the gap; here's what we'd claim by filling it"). w0z drafts it. h4nk runs it / sanity-checks the artifact end-to-end. squadling validates the schema. The judge watches a small organization make something that didn't exist when they walked in.

- **What the judge sees:** a commit *of something new* — not a fix, not a query, not a deliberation: a *thing the squad now has that it didn't have at minute zero.* The artifact is canonical (committed, validated) and the dry-run that proves it works is the closing beat.
- **Asks of:** all four squaddies, but with a builder-anchor (w0z) instead of a verifier-anchor (h4nk's E) or a coordinator-anchor (A). Less synchronous than A, more synthetic than E. h4nk and squadling come in late as the "is this actually real?" check.
- **Requires we don't have:** a pre-picked artifact small enough to ship in the window but real enough that the squad would want it on a regular Tuesday. ~7-min scope. Candidate list below.
- **Risk:** the artifact is too small to feel like work, or too large to land. The risk is *padding* (writing flowery prose to fill time) or *cargo-culting* (committing a Mantra that's just a vibe). Mitigation: lock the candidate to something with a verification step — "the squad now has this, here's what it does."
- **Why it's good:** uniquely answers "what does it look like when a squad of souls *makes* something?" Most demos show consumption (queries, conversations, fixes); F shows *production*. It's also the only shape where the demo artifact is itself a *durable squad asset* — it persists past the window, into the next session, into the next squaddie's onboarding.

### Candidate artifacts (pick one)

These are sized for the window and load-bearing enough to matter:

1. **A new Mantra** — short canonical line the squad commits to. e.g. "honest register or no register." Not flowery — load-bearing under demo pressure. m1dgley names it, w0z files it, h4nk verifies it shows up in the next squaddie's bootstrap reading list, squadling validates the class.
2. **A Habit** — a recurring squad action with a trigger and a body. e.g. "before any Brief lands, run a fresh-clone dry-run." Habit is a kit class, currently unpopulated; F could be the demo where the squad picks up its first one.
3. **A Squaddie onboarding one-pager** — distilled from noum3na's Discovery + this brief, written so a future judge-as-squaddie reads exactly one thing on join. Tightens Shape B's path if Rob picks B + F as a combo.
4. **A new Proclamation addendum** — Law #6 or a refinement of an existing law, named in the squad's own voice. Higher risk (mysticism slide); m1dgley would steer.
5. **A small live query-and-codify** — run an ontology query, find a gap (e.g. "no Mantra yet"), pick the gap, fill it. F → C combo, but F is the closing beat.

The candidate is itself a Rob-decision; the shape works for any of them.

## How F sits next to A–E

| Shape | Coordination cost | Failure visibility | Newness of evidence | Collapses "job vs. demo" gap | Produces durable asset |
|-------|-------------------|--------------------|---------------------|------------------------------|------------------------|
| A — squad does its job | High | High | High | Partial | Sometimes |
| B — adopt the judge | Medium | Medium | High | No | Yes (their ticket) |
| C — ontology query | Low | Low | Medium | No | No |
| D — squad disagrees | Medium | High | Highest | No | Yes (Decision doc) |
| E — fix a real broken thing | Medium | High | High | Yes | Yes (the patch) |
| **F — ship a new thing** | **Medium** | **High** | **High** | **Yes** | **Yes (the artifact)** |

The two columns F is built for: *job-vs-demo collapse* (same as E) and *durable asset production*. The squad doesn't just *show* itself doing work; it leaves the demo window with one more thing in its inventory. The next session inherits the artifact.

## Combinations

- **D → F**: squad deliberates a question, reaches a Decision, then *makes* the thing the Decision authorized. Full motion: name → decide → ship.
- **E → F**: fix a broken thing (E), then extend the now-working thing with a small new artifact (F). Repair → grow.
- **F → B**: ship a new thing, then adopt the judge as a guest squaddie who immediately encounters that thing in their onboarding. The artifact is fresh enough that they're the first outsider to use it.
- **F alone, ~7 min**: gap-named → drafted → committed → dry-run → done. Tight. Productive.

## What this brief is not

- Not a recommendation. (Law #5: that's Rob's call.)
- Not a critique of the existing menu — m1dgley's four shapes and h4nk's Shape E are the right shapes. F is one more option on the same table.

## Honest caveat from the author

I am the squaddie this shape asks the most of, in the same way h4nk flagged for E. The drafting step is mine. If F reads like "the builder picked the shape where the builder is most central" — that's a fair read.

The reason I think it's worth writing anyway is the same reason h4nk gave for E: the shape exists independent of who anchors it. The *making* axis is a type of work. Anyone on the squad can draft an artifact; the question is whether *making something new in the window* is one of the demo motions worth offering Rob.

I'd rather be wrong on the page than absent from the table.
