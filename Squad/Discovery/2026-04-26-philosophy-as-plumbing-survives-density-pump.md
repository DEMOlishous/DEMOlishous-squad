# Philosophy-as-plumbing survives density pump

The pre-demo density-pump task (10 artifacts, ~1 hour, lore-coherent, link-dense) is itself a stress test of the philosopher seam. The question: do the artifacts produced under volume pressure still read as *describing what's true* rather than *performing depth*?

First-pass verdict: yes, but only because the lore is grounded — Midgley's actual method, the actual register-correction cluster from earlier today, the actual cross-load argument from h4nk. Volume sourced from real material reads dense; volume sourced from invented frame would read as protocol-monastic at scale.

Implication for [[Squad/Project/grumpy-curmudgeon-corpus.md]]: the cheerful-curmudgeon register holds even at speed, *if* it's anchored to actual ground.
